My Favorite Projects

  • FIRST Robotics Competition Data Analysis

    - When tasked with an open-ended data science project designed to show off what I had learned, choosing to focus on the 2022 FIRST Robotics Competition World Championships was an easy decision following 8 years of involvement with my team. Using my team's hand-collected scouting data, I put together an analysis of the teams in our division with two main objectives: attempting to predict teams' final rankings and classifying teams based on skill and performance. Check out a few of my visualizations!

  • Women in Data Science 2023 Datathon

    - In January 2023, I competed in the Women in Data Science 2023 Climate Change Datathon. My partner and I experimented with a wide variety of machine learning models in order to contrast the strengths of different techniques and the effects of data cleaning and regularization. We experimented with Linear Regression, Lasso Regression, XGBoost, and Principal Components Analysis, to name just a few of our techniques.

  • Higher Ground Youth & Family Services Halloween Costume Project

    - Through Chapman University's partnership with Higher Ground Youth & Family Services after-school program, my project team was partnered with a young boy whose teachers had nominated him to receive a custom-made, 3D printed Halloween Costume to bring some joy into his difficult home life. To fulfill his wish of being a Stormtrooper for Halloween, we created a Stormtrooper blaster, helmet, and candy bucket. Pushing ourselves to our limits, we also created an electronic lightsaber complete with animations and the ability to change colors at the press of a button.

  • 3D Modeling and Printing the Monado

    - For my most ambitious project yet, I created my own 3D model of the Monado from Xenoblade Chronicles and brought it to life. Held together by magnets, the printed sword is just over two feet long. The sword is equipped with an interchangeable laser-cut disk to represent the sword's current form, and I plan on adding electronics and lights to the next iteration!
